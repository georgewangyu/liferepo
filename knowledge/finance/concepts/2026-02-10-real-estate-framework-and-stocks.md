


# Real Estate & Infrastructure Sector Framework

## 1. Homebuilders (Residential Developers)

### Core Macro Drivers
- Mortgage rates
- Fed rate policy / rate cut expectations
- Housing inventory levels
- Employment and wage growth
- New home order growth

### Key Stocks

- **DHI – D.R. Horton**: Largest U.S. homebuilder, broad national exposure.
- **LEN – Lennar**: Major volume builder, strong balance sheet.
- **PHM – PulteGroup**: Mid-to-high-end exposure, strong cash flow discipline.
- **TOL – Toll Brothers**: Luxury homebuilder, sensitive to high-income demand.
- **TMHC – Taylor Morrison**: Focused on move-up and entry-level buyers.
- **MTH – Meritage Homes**: Entry-level focus, benefits from affordability trends.
- **KBH – KB Home**: Customization-focused builder with regional exposure.
- **GRBK – Green Brick Partners**: Growth-focused regional builder.
- **MRP – Millrose Properties**: Smaller-cap residential exposure, more volatile and cyclical.
- **MHO – M/I Homes**: Mid-sized homebuilder with strong regional concentration and higher beta characteristics.

### Sector Characteristics
- Highly interest-rate sensitive
- Strongly cyclical
- Can move sharply on rate expectations
- Often front-runs Fed pivot narratives

## 2. Heavy Construction & Infrastructure

### Core Macro Drivers
- Federal infrastructure spending
- Energy grid upgrades
- Data center and AI power expansion
- LNG and energy investment cycles
- Government stimulus programs

### Key Stocks

- **PWR – Quanta Services**: Power grid and energy infrastructure contractor.
- **MTZ – MasTec**: Energy and telecom infrastructure builder.
- **J – Jacobs Solutions**: Engineering and consulting for large infrastructure projects.
- **ACM – AECOM**: Infrastructure design and program management.
- **STRL – Sterling Infrastructure**: Civil and transportation infrastructure.
- **PRIM – Primoris Services**: Energy and utility infrastructure contractor.
- **FLR – Fluor Corporation**: Large-scale industrial and engineering construction projects.
- **ROAD – Construction Partners**: Asphalt and road construction focused contractor.
- **CAT – Caterpillar**: Heavy machinery manufacturer leveraged to global construction cycles.

### Sector Characteristics
- Longer project cycles than homebuilders
- More dependent on government and industrial capital expenditure
- Typically lower beta than homebuilders
- Often benefits from multi-year investment themes (grid, energy transition, AI infrastructure)

## Advanced Entry Logic – 4 Structural Questions

When evaluating any stock in homebuilders, infrastructure, or AI-related sectors, apply these four structural questions before entering:

1️⃣ **Are we at structural lows, or has price already rallied significantly?**
- If near weekly support or early base breakout → better risk/reward
- If extended 15–25% in a short period → higher pullback risk

2️⃣ **Is this a breakout phase, or a blow-off / rejection move?**
- Clean breakout: tight consolidation → strong expansion
- Fake breakout: long upper wicks, immediate rejection

3️⃣ **Is volume expanding on the breakout?**
- True breakouts show clear volume expansion
- Low-volume moves are more likely to fade

4️⃣ **Is this a weekly timeframe initiation or daily timeframe exhaustion?**
- Weekly breakout = multi-week swing potential
- Late-stage daily extension = short-term trade only

# Decision Scoring Model (Entry Only If Score ≥ 3)

This model converts the framework into a mechanical decision filter.
No discretionary overrides.

---

## Step 1 – Macro Alignment (0 or 1 point)

Does the macro backdrop support the sector?

- Homebuilders → Rate cut expectations rising? Mortgage rates falling?
- Infrastructure → Government spending / grid capex narrative active?
- AI → Strong AI capex / earnings momentum?

If YES → +1
If unclear / neutral → 0
If macro contradicts thesis → DO NOT TRADE

---

## Step 2 – Structural Position (0 or 1 point)

Where is price relative to structure?

- Early breakout from base
- Weekly higher low forming
- Near major support with defined invalidation

Extended vertical move (>15–20% in short time) = 0
Clear early-stage structure = +1

---

## Step 3 – Breakout Quality & Volume (0 or 1 point)

Is the move supported by participation?

- Breakout above prior high with volume expansion → +1
- Breakout on weak volume or long upper wicks → 0
- Fake breakout / rejection → DO NOT TRADE

---

## Step 4 – Timeframe Alignment (0 or 1 point)

Are timeframes aligned?

- Weekly + Daily both trending → +1
- Weekly flat but Daily extended → 0
- Daily strong but Weekly resistance overhead → 0

---

## Step 5 – Risk Definition (Mandatory Gate)

Before entry, answer:

"If I am wrong, where is structure invalidated?"

- Clear invalidation level defined → proceed
- No invalidation → DO NOT TRADE

(This step does not add points but is required.)
