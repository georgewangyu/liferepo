

# Trading Foundations & Learning Framework

> Purpose: Turn scattered information into a **coherent trading system**. This document defines what to focus on, what to ignore, and how knowledge connects to execution.

---

## 1. Current Goal & Constraints

### Primary objective (short–term)
- Generate returns through **short-term / day trading**
- Focus on **execution, repeatability, and risk control**, not prediction

### Constraints acknowledged
- Limited attention bandwidth → cannot track all factors
- Markets are noisy → simplicity beats complexity
- Broker behavior, liquidity, and execution matter as much as direction

---

## 2. What “Technical Analysis” Actually Is (Decomposed)

Technical analysis is not one thing. It is a **stack of modules**, each with a different job.

### 2.1 Price Structure (Highest Priority)
- Trend: up / down / range
- Market structure: HH / HL / LH / LL
- Support & resistance
- Breakouts vs failed breakouts
- Ranges (consolidation)

> Rule: No trade without a clear price structure thesis.

---

### 2.2 Volume & Liquidity (Non‑Negotiable)
- Volume expansion vs contraction
- Breakouts that are supported by volume
- Bid/Ask spread
- Liquidity depth (can I exit?)

> Direction without liquidity is theoretical profit only.

---

### 2.3 Trend Confirmation Tools (Secondary)
- EMA / SMA (trend filter, not signal generator)
- VWAP (critical for intraday context)

> Indicators confirm context; they do not create edge by themselves.

---

### 2.4 Momentum & Overextension (Tertiary)
- RSI
- MACD / Stochastic (optional, not required)

> Use to avoid bad entries, not to predict reversals.

---

### 2.5 Time & Event Structure
- Opening range (first 30 minutes)
- Midday liquidity drop
- Power hour
- Earnings, macro data, news catalysts

> Same pattern behaves differently at different times.

---

## 3. What to Explicitly Ignore (for Now)

To prevent overfitting and paralysis, the following are **out of scope**:
- Excessive indicators
- Complex harmonic patterns
- Fibonacci retracements
- Indicator stacking without logic
- “It worked last time” reasoning

> Edge comes from clarity, not complexity.

---

## 4. Minimal Viable Day Trading Setup (MVP)

### Charts
- 5m + 1m candles
- Volume
- VWAP
- One EMA (9 or 20)
- Optional RSI

### Decision rule
> If I cannot explain the trade in **one sentence**, I do not take it.

Examples:
- “Trend continuation with VWAP pullback and volume confirmation”
- “Range low rejection with volume exhaustion”
- “Failed breakout with acceptance back into range”

---

## 5. Learning Strategy (Systematic, Not Random)

### Phase 1 — Foundations
- Price action & market structure
- Support / resistance
- Volume basics

### Phase 2 — Execution & Context
- VWAP usage
- Time-of-day behavior
- Entry & exit mechanics

### Phase 3 — Refinement
- Trade journaling
- Pattern statistics
- Emotional & execution errors

> Learning is only valid if it feeds back into execution.

---

## 6. Knowledge → Rules → Behavior Loop

1. Learn concept
2. Translate into a rule
3. Apply in live or sim
4. Journal outcome
5. Adjust rules

> Information that does not become a rule is noise.

---

## 7. Identity & Operating Principles

- I am not here to catch every move
- I prioritize survival and repeatability
- Missing trades is cheaper than forcing trades
- My edge is discipline, not prediction
